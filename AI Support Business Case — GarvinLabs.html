01
You're about to hire someone to fix the support backlog
A support hire costs ₹4–12L/year plus management overhead. The backlog came from volume, not headcount. One more person doesn't solve the classification problem.
→ Open "customer support", "CRM executive", or "customer happiness" role on LinkedIn right now
02
Your support failures are already public
Trustpilot, MouthShut, IndiaCustomerCare.com — customers don't wait on a slow inbox. They post. At ₹5Cr revenue, that's not an ops problem. It's a brand problem.
→ Reviews mentioning "no reply", "called and no one answered", "emailed twice with zero response"
03
Your team-to-revenue ratio doesn't add up
3 people processing 4 lakh orders. 36 people running ₹100Cr+ revenue. At some point, the math stops working — and support breaks first because it's fully reactive.
→ Revenue or order volume growing significantly faster than team headcount
04
You run subscription — and the inbox multiplies every cycle
Every renewal generates the same tickets: WISMO, pause/skip/cancel, billing queries. Subscription volume is predictable, recurring, and entirely automatable — if the system can recognise it before a human opens it.
→ 10,000+ active subscribers, flat support team
05
You scaled fast and support never caught up
17x headcount in 12 months. 109% revenue CAGR. International markets. Every system lags at this pace — but support is the one customers notice immediately.
→ New geographies or channels added with no dedicated support ops infrastructure
06
Multi-channel means multi-type tickets in one flat inbox
D2C website WISMO differs from Nykaa returns, which differ from Amazon complaints. When four channels route into one inbox with no classification, resolution time doubles on every ticket.
→ Selling across own site + 2 or more marketplaces with no tagging or routing system
07
Your product complexity generates a predictable FAQ flood
Cloth diapers (sizing, wash count, returns). Ayurveda (ingredients, dosage, customisation). Subscriptions (refill timing, plan changes). These are predictable ticket types — and predictable means classifiable.
→ High rate of pre-purchase and post-purchase FAQ tickets from product complexity

Implementation — Rollout

Live in 2–3 weeks.
Four stages, no big bang.

No switching helpdesks. No replacing your team overnight. Each stage is validated before the next begins — you see it working at every step.

1
Classification only — AI reads, humans still respond
The system tags every incoming ticket (WISMO, returns, billing, urgent, FAQ) before anyone opens it. Your team opens a pre-sorted inbox instead of a flat unread queue. Zero risk — AI sees, doesn't touch responses yet.
2
Assisted responses — AI drafts, human approves before send
For repetitive ticket types, the system drafts a reply and holds for human review. One click to approve. Your team reviews instead of writes from scratch. You validate accuracy before enabling automation.
3
Low-risk auto-handling — repetitive tier goes fully automated
After Stage 2 validates the responses, high-confidence policy and FAQ categories are automated — replies sent instantly from your knowledge base. This is where the 61% auto-resolution rate was demonstrated in our test deployment. Shopify order lookup for live WISMO replies is built into this stage.
4
Expanded coverage — new categories added monthly
As confidence data accumulates, new ticket types are added to the automated tier. Monthly reports show auto-resolution rate, hours saved, and the next expansion candidates. Retainer covers all ongoing optimisation.

What's needed to start: shipping / returns / cancellation policy docs, 5–10 example tickets, escalation rules, and read access to your inbox. Build starts within 48 hours of receiving those.
