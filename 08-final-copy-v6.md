### What a Diagnostic output looks like

*Manufacturing client, India · Operations transformation*

A manufacturing business running two plants was losing 30–40 days per job to three invisible failures: no job tracking, no documentation discipline at point of work, and no minimum stock enforcement. Nobody could see the problem until a job was already late.

The Diagnostic mapped 26 business processes, identified 7 root-cause issues, and traced the delay chain from raw material procurement through to payment receipt. The output wasn't a list of recommendations — it was a specific, sequenced roadmap with four automation opportunities, each scoped to build time and monthly dollar value:

- Job Status Dashboard: 4–6h build. Replaces 1–2 hours of manual Director review daily. Converts invisible delays into visible ones before they cascade.
- Payment Clock Tracker: 3–4h build. Automated weekly payment status with escalation at Day 40 and Day 45. Directly reduces cash flow delay on every job.
- Material Reorder Alert: 2–3h build. Stock level check against reorder table, alert to Purchase when below threshold. Eliminates a 9–10 day delay that was hitting every single job at the same point.
- Email Template Automation: 4–5h build. Seven pre-populated templates (PO acknowledgement, dispatch advice, GRN follow-up, payment reminder). Zero-effort audit trail for Sales.

Total automation effort across all four: 14–18 hours of build time.

The business case in the Diagnostic: cutting cycle time from 60 days to 35 days on the same physical capacity means running 1.7x more jobs per year without hiring, without new machines, and without new orders.

The client received this as a written document plus a 20-minute Loom walkthrough, delivered 72 hours after the discovery call. The build recommendation was prioritised so the highest-ROI fix (job visibility) shipped first, in week one, before any automation was written.
