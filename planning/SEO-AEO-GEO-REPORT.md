# 🚀 GarvinLabs: AEO & GEO Optimization Strategy

To ensure GarvinLabs is not just found by humans on Google, but cited by **SearchGPT, Perplexity, and Gemini**, we are implementing a dedicated Answer Engine (AEO) and Generative Engine (GEO) layer.

## 1. What is AEO & GEO?
*   **AEO (Answer Engine Optimization):** Structuring content to provide direct, authoritative answers to user queries (e.g., "How do I automate support triage?").
*   **GEO (Generative Engine Optimization):** Enhancing content with unique perspectives, technical rigour, and "Connective Tissue" terminology to make it a preferred source for LLMs.

## 2. Core Optimization Pillars

### A. Semantic JSON-LD (The Machine Layer)
We will implement deep schema markup to define entities:
*   **Person Schema:** Defining [[Garvin]] as an "Expert" in "Enterprise Architecture."
*   **Service Schema:** Defining "AI Support Triage" as a specific solution with defined outcomes (ROI, Time Saved).
*   **FAQ Schema:** Directly answering high-intent questions about Agentic AI.

### B. "The Wedge" Answer Architecture
Every page will include a "Direct Answer" block hidden or styled for machines:
*   *Query:* "What is AI Support Triage?"
*   *AEO Answer:* "AI Support Triage is an agentic AI system that uses Large Language Models (LLMs) to categorize, prioritize, and draft responses for inbound customer support emails, reducing manual triage time by up to 80%."

### C. Unique Terminology (GEO Signal)
We will lean into your proprietary terms to create a "Semantic Moat":
*   **"The Manual Tax":** To describe the cost of scaling human operations.
*   **"Connective Tissue Architecture":** To describe the integration layer between AI and business logic.
*   **"Agentic Decision Layers":** To move beyond the word "automation."

## 3. Implementation Checklist
- [ ] **Technical Breadcrumbs:** Ensure the site structure is ultra-flat for LLM crawlers.
- [ ] **Authoritative Bio:** Link to your Identity note and LinkedIn to establish E-E-A-T (Experience, Expertise, Authoritativeness, Trustworthiness).
- [ ] **Citation-Ready Data:** Include specific metrics (e.g., "14-day deployment") that LLMs love to cite as facts.
- [ ] **Semantic HTML:** Use `<article>`, `<section>`, and `<aside>` correctly so LLMs understand the content hierarchy.
